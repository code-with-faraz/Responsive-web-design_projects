Alan Rickman

A distinguished English actor, captivated audiences with his mesmerizing performances and iconic roles, from the enigmatic Severus Snape in Harry Potter to the charismatic Hans Gruber in Die Hard.

Here's a time line of Alan Rickman's Life:

Early Life

  • February 21, 1946: Alan Sidney Patrick Rickman is born in Acton, London, England.
  • 1951: Rickman's father, Bernard Rickman, dies of lung cancer when Alan is just 8 years old.
  • 1965: Rickman wins a scholarship to the Latymer Upper School in London.
  • Late 1960s: Attends Chelsea College of Art and Design and later the Royal College of Art.

Career Beginnings

  • 1972: Joins the Royal Shakespeare Company.
  • 1978: Appears in "Romeo and Juliet" with the Royal Shakespeare Company.

Breakthrough and Rise to Fame

  • 1985: Gains acclaim for his role as the Vicomte de Valmont in the stage production of "Les Liaisons Dangereuses."
  • 1988: Makes his Hollywood debut as Hans Gruber in "Die Hard," earning international recognition.

Film and Theater Highlights

  • 1991: Plays the Sheriff of Nottingham in "Robin Hood: Prince of Thieves," winning a BAFTA Award.
  • 1995: Stars as Colonel Brandon in "Sense and Sensibility."
  • 1996: Directs his first film, "The Winter Guest."
  • 1999: Portrays Metatron in "Dogma" and Alexander Dane in "Galaxy Quest."
  • 2001: Begins his iconic role as Severus Snape in the "Harry Potter" film series.
  • 2010: Stars in the film "Alice in Wonderland" as the voice of the Caterpillar.

Later Life and Legacy

  • 2015: Appears in his final film roles in "Eye in the Sky" and "Alice Through the Looking Glass."
  • January 14, 2016: Passes away from pancreatic cancer at the age of 69 in London.

Posthumous Recognition

  • 2016: Receives various tributes and posthumous awards, including the Annie Award for Voice Acting in "Alice Through the Looking Glass."
